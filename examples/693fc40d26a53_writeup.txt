SECTION 8 — BIOLOGICAL INTERPRETATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  1. G-QUADRUPLEX CLUSTER (~12,300–12,900 bp)
     Four G4 candidates occur within 600 bp.  Co-occurring G4 + R-Loop in the
     same zone is a known feature of gene promoters and replication-stressed
     regions.  The 12 bp CpG run (CGCGCGCGCGCG) at pos 12,488 immediately
     within this band may represent a CpG island boundary, further supporting
     a gene regulatory context.

  2. REPEAT-RICH ZONE (~3,400–6,200 bp)
     A convergence of dinucleotide microsatellites (AC, AT, TA), trinucleotide
     repeats (AAT, AAA), and long A/T homopolymer runs.  This density is
     characteristic of tandemly repeated satellite-like DNA, potentially within
     an intronic or intergenic repeat region.  The co-occurrence of Curved DNA
     and Triplex motifs suggests this zone can adopt bent or three-stranded
     conformations under torsional stress.

  3. POLY-A / POLY-T RUNS
     11 poly-A runs (≥10 bp) and 9 poly-T runs (≥10 bp) are distributed
     throughout the sequence.  The largest (34× poly-A at pos 6055) could
     represent a simple sequence repeat (SSR) locus.  These are classical
     slippage-prone elements associated with replication errors and
     frameshift mutations (if in coding context).

  4. SINGLE i-MOTIF CANDIDATE (pos 24,153)
     A single high-quality i-motif site with 4 cytosine tracts and short loops
     (2 bp).  i-Motifs form on the cytosine-rich strand complementary to G4;
     this site is ~11,900 bp away from the G4 hotspot, suggesting they are
     independent elements rather than complementary partners.

  5. MODERATE GC CONTENT (51.9 %)
     The near-equal GC/AT balance is consistent with a euchromatic gene body
     or gene-adjacent region.  This GC level supports G4 and Z-DNA formation
     while still allowing abundant AT-rich repeat structures.
